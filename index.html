Summary of the Life of Nelson Mandela

Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela was a South African anti-apartheid activist and politician who served as the first president of South Africa from 1994 to 1999. He was the country's first black head of state and the first elected in a fully representative democratic election.

  • Born: July 18, 1918
  • Died: December 5, 2013 (age 95)
  • Awards: Nobel Peace Prize, Bharat Ratna, and many more
  • Presidential term: May 10, 1994 – June 16, 1999
  • 1918–1934: Childhood in the kingdom of Thembu, where his grandfather was king.
  • 1934–1940: Began secondary education at Clarkebury Methodist High School aiming to serve as a royal councillor.
  • 1941–1943: Worked as a night watchman at Crown Mines — his first glimpse of South African capitalism in action.
  • 1943–1949: Studied law and joined the ANC Youth League.
  • 1950–1954: Elected national president of the ANCYL and joined the ANC national executive.
  • 1955–1961: Congress of the People and the Treason Trial.
  • 1962–1964: Arrested during the Rivonia Trial; sentenced to life imprisonment.
  • 1964–1982: Imprisoned on Robben Island.
  • 1982–1988: Transferred to Pollsmoor Prison, Cape Town.
  • 1988–1990: Released from Victor Verster Prison on February 11, 1990.
